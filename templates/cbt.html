Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is a goal-oriented psychotherapy that teaches you to recognize and restructure unhelpful thoughts, leading to healthier emotions and behaviors.

How CBT Works

CBT is usually delivered in 6–12 structured weekly sessions. Working with a therapist, you’ll:

  • Identify automatic negative thoughts (ANTs)
  • Challenge cognitive distortions
  • Replace them with balanced, evidence-based thoughts
  • Practice new behaviors through real-life homework

Benefits

  • Reduces PTSD, anxiety, and depression symptoms
  • Teaches lifelong coping skills you can reuse
  • Structured and time-limited—progress is measurable

Self-Help Tips

  • Keep a thought diary: record triggering situations, thoughts, and feelings
  • Use the “ABCDE” method (Activate, Belief, Consequence, Dispute, Effect)
  • Practice gradual exposure to feared situations
  • Reward yourself for small behavioral wins
