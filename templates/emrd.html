EMDR Therapy

Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing (EMDR) is a proven, trauma-focused approach that uses rhythmic eye movements or other bilateral stimulation to help the brain reprocess distressing memories, reducing their emotional impact.

How EMDR Works

EMDR follows an 8-phase protocol that guides you from history-taking to future resilience. Each phase targets a different aspect of your healing journey.

Phase 1 – History & Treatment Planning

Your therapist reviews your background, identifies target memories, and develops a roadmap for the sessions ahead.

Phase 2 – Preparation

You learn coping techniques (like safe-place visualization) to manage emotional distress during reprocessing.

Phase 3 – Assessment

Specific target memories, negative beliefs, and desired positive beliefs are defined.

Phase 4 – Desensitization

Guided sets of eye movements (or taps/sounds) help your brain reprocess the disturbing memory until distress subsides.

Phase 5 – Installation

The positive belief you chose becomes associated with the now-neutralized memory.

Phase 6 – Body Scan

You notice and clear any lingering physical tension linked to the memory.

Phase 7 – Closure

Every session ends with grounding techniques to ensure you leave feeling safe and stable.

Phase 8 – Reevaluation

Your therapist reviews progress, checks remaining triggers, and plans future targets.

Key Benefits

Rapid relief from trauma symptoms compared to traditional talk therapy.
Reduces flashbacks, nightmares, and intrusive thoughts.
Requires minimal verbal detail; less re-traumatization.
Promotes healthy neural reconnection and adaptive beliefs.

Self-Care Between Sessions

  • Practice grounding techniques (e.g., 5-4-3-2-1 senses exercise).
  • Keep a journal to track emotional shifts.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain balanced nutrition.
  • Reach out to supportive friends, family, or your therapist if distress intensifies.
  • Prioritize sleep and gentle movement (walks, stretching).
